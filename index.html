About

Academic profile

My academic journey combines teaching, applied research, mentorship, and innovation in artificial intelligence. I earned my Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Mysore, Karnataka-India, with research centered on machine learning for chronic obstructive pulmonary disease diagnosis from chest X-ray images.

At DSU, I aim to build bridges between computer science, medical engineering, clinical needs, and socially useful AI systems. I am especially interested in methods that are not only accurate, but also explainable, deployable, ethical, and useful in real settings.

Research

Focus areas and current directions

My research is driven by the belief that AI should improve human health, dignity, accessibility, and institutional capability.

▣

AI for Medical Imaging

Deep learning and explainable models for COPD diagnosis, diabetic retinopathy, breast cancer thermography, pneumonia, PET-CT analysis, radiomics, and clinical decision support.

◎

Infant Biometrics

AI-assisted newborn footprint and palmprint recognition for reliable infant identity assurance, hospital safety, and low-cost deployable biometric workflows.

✦

Assistive Technology

Adaptive AAC systems, tele-consultation tools for SLPs and audiologists, and AI solutions that support children with cerebral palsy and communication needs.

⌌

Quantum ML for Healthcare

Completed a draft cookbook on Quantum Machine Learning for Medical Image Analysis, focused on recipes, code patterns, and research pathways for early-career researchers.
